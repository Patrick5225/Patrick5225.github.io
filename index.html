About Me

Hi! My name is Patrick. I graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) with a Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics on June 2020. I also graduated from Bakersfield College with an Associate of Science in Computer Science.

During my undergraduate years, I have been involved in multiple extracurriculars. I became president, secretary and vice president of a math club from Bakersfield College. In our club, we have worked together on group projects relating to math and statistics and presented in several math conferences, including the Joint Mathematics Meetings (JMM) at San Diego and MREC^3 at CSU Northridge. I also got a chance to speak in a student panel for one of the math conferences that I attended.

I received multiple awards including an outstanding student award from my physics class. I also received recommendations from multiple math professors for inspiring other students with determiniation to learn and succeed, that it lead me to becoming employed as an academic math tutor at Bakersfield college. Topics I tutored included subjects such as calculus and linear algebra.

I love being able to solve problems and utilize my critical thinking skills. I committed myself to learn about statistics and being able to analyze results that are meaningful to others. My ultimate goal is to become an analyst who can help others make useful and important decisions.
