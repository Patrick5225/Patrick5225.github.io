About Me

Hi! My name is Patrick. I graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) with a Bachelor's of Science in Applied Mathematics. I also graduated from Bakersfield College with an Associate's in Science in Computer Science. Throughout my undergraduate career, I have taken courses useful and relevant in the field of data analytics. I created math and statistics projects in a team and presented in several math conferences, including the Joint Mathematics Meetings (JMM) at San Diego and MREC^3 at CSU Northridge. You can check out one of our Powerpoint presentations that we presented and created which discusses the fallacies of statistics being misused with data dredging in research and how the need for qualified statisticians are in need. I was also employed as an academic math tutor to help other students succeed in their math classes.

After graduation, I spent my time with self-development by learning in-demand skills needed for data analytics. I then took what I learned to practice solving problems and work on analytic projects. My ultimate goal is to become a data analyst who can help others make useful and important decisions, and eventually work my way up to become a Senior data analyst.
